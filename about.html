Kimball Germane

I am a research associate at the University of Utah School of Computing. In the fall, I will begin a Ph.D. in Computer Science advised by Matt Might.

In December 2012, I received an M.S. in Computer Science from Brigham Young University. For my thesis, I constructed a semantics-preserving transformation for continuation marks. My advisor was Jay McCarthy.

Before working with Jay McCarthy, I studied and Internet Security. I was one of four members of BYU's winning team in the 2011 MITRE STEM CTF.

Prior to that, I studied Statistics and Mathematics, and received a B.S. in Mathematics with a specialized minor in Computer Science, also from BYU.
